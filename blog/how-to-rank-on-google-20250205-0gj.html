How To Rank On Google

A Casual Guide to Not Getting Lost in the Algorithm Sauce

Alright, so you wanna rank on Google, huh? Cool, cool. But let's be real—Google's algorithm is like that one friend who's always changing their mind. One day they're into avocado toast, the next day it's all about matcha lattes. You gotta keep up, or you'll get left behind.

1. Know What Google Wants (Spoiler: It's Not Just Keywords)

Back in the day, ranking on Google was like playing a game of Mad Libs. Stuff your page with keywords, and boom—you're on the first page. But now? Google's got this thing called E-E-A-T (Expertise, Experience, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness). Yeah, it's a mouthful, but it basically means Google wants to know you're not just some rando spamming the internet.

2. Content Is King (But It's Also the Whole Damn Kingdom)

Here's the deal: if your content sucks, you're not ranking. Period. Google's all about giving users what they want, so if your blog post reads like it was written by a robot who's never met a human, you're toast.

  • Write for humans, not bots
  • Answer questions people are actually asking
  • Make it skimmable

3. Backlinks Are Like Social Proof for Your Website

Imagine you're at a party, and someone's like, "Oh, you have to meet Sarah. She's amazing." That's what backlinks are—other websites vouching for you. The more high-quality sites linking to you, the more Google thinks, "Hmm, this person must be legit."

4. Speed Matters (Like, a Lot)

If your website loads slower than a dial-up connection in 1998, you're not ranking. Google's all about user experience, and nobody's sticking around for a site that takes forever to load.

  • Optimize your images
  • Use a decent hosting provider
  • Minify your code

5. Mobile-Friendly Is Non-Negotiable

Let's be honest—most people are Googling stuff on their phones while they're supposed to be working. If your site looks like a hot mess on mobile, you're not ranking.

6. Don't Sleep on Technical SEO

Okay, I know "technical SEO" sounds boring, but it's kinda like the plumbing of your website. If it's messed up, everything else falls apart.

7. Be Patient (Yeah, I Know, It Sucks)

Here's the thing: ranking on Google doesn't happen overnight. It's more like a slow burn. You gotta put in the work, wait for the algorithm to do its thing, and hope for the best.

Final Thoughts

Ranking on Google isn't rocket science, but it's not exactly a walk in the park either. It's a mix of good content, solid technical stuff, and a little bit of luck. So, what are you waiting for? Go fix that slow-loading site, write some killer content, and start building those backlinks.
